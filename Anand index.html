Graduate Capstone · Franklin University · BUSA-695

Patient Analytics — HW Dental Group

Applied unsupervised and supervised machine learning to de-identified patient data (n=4,546) across four Columbus-area dental branches to identify structural disparities in insurance access, Medicaid concentration, and digital engagement.

Segmentation
K-Means (k=4) — identified four patient archetypes with distinct insurance, Medicaid, and digital profiles. Silhouette score: 0.32.
Classification
Logistic Regression + Random Forest — predicted uninsured status with 79.7% accuracy (AUC 0.698). Branch location was the strongest predictor (OR=1.693).
Trend Analysis
Time Series (13 months) — Medicaid share increased from ~30% to ~47% while volume grew +8.2/month, indicating declining revenue per patient.
Key finding

Branch location is the primary driver of insurance disparity. A single network-wide strategy is insufficient — each branch requires a differentiated intervention plan.

04
Analytical approach

Methodology

De-identified data from 4,546 patients across four branches was cleaned, feature-engineered, and analyzed using three complementary methods: K-Means clustering (k=4) for unsupervised patient segmentation, Logistic Regression and Random Forest for binary classification of uninsured status (5-fold CV, class-balanced), and NumPy polyfit for 13-month payer-mix trend detection.

Strategic recommendations

Six model-grounded interventions — each backed by specific model evidence, a measurable KPI, and a realistic timeline. Interventions prioritized by revenue impact and data readiness.
